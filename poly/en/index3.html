Basic Grammar (A1 - A2)

The Present Simple is used to express general truths, habits, and routines. Example: "I go to school every day."

The Present Simple is commonly used to talk about daily habits and routines.
  • I wake up at 7:00 a.m. every day.
  • She drinks coffee in the morning.
  • We go to the gym three times a week.
  • He plays football on Sundays.
  • They study English every afternoon.
These sentences describe habitual actions that happen regularly, often with adverbs like "always," "usually," or "never."

In the workplace, the Present Simple is used to describe regular tasks or duties.
  • I answer emails every morning.
  • She attends meetings on Mondays.
  • They work on the project every Friday.
  • We start work at 9 a.m. every day.
  • He checks the inventory twice a week.
These examples show how the Present Simple can describe repetitive tasks.

The Present Simple can also describe permanent states or characteristics.
  • She is very friendly and helpful.
  • He always stays calm under pressure.
  • They are creative and work well together.
  • I am very organized and punctual.
  • We are motivated to achieve our goals.
These sentences talk about qualities and personal traits.

The Present Simple is often used to state facts or general truths.
  • The Earth orbits the Sun.
  • Water boils at 100°C.
  • Cats are independent animals.
  • The sun rises in the east.
  • Light travels faster than sound.
These statements are factual and remain true over time.

The Present Simple has specific rules for regular and irregular verbs, as well as special rules for the third person singular.
  • For regular verbs: add **-s** or **-es** in the third person singular (he, she, it). Example: He **works** at a hospital.
  • Negative form: Use **do not** (don't) or **does not** (doesn't) for third person singular. Example: They **do not** play tennis.
  • Question form: Use **do** or **does** at the beginning of a question. Example: **Does** she speak English?

Try these exercises to test your knowledge of the Present Simple:
  1. Complete the sentence: "She ___ (go) to the gym every morning."
  2. Form a negative sentence: "I do not ___ (like) chocolate."
  3. Ask a question: "___ (you / like) to read books?"
  4. Form a sentence using "he" and "swim."
  5. Change this sentence to negative: "They eat lunch at 12 p.m."

The Present Continuous is used to describe actions happening right now or temporary actions. Example: "I am studying English."

The Past Simple is used for completed actions in the past. Example: "I visited London last year."

The Future Simple is used to express actions that will happen in the future. Example: "I will travel to Paris next summer."

Subject pronouns are used to replace the subject of a sentence. Examples: "I, you, he, she, it, we, they."
